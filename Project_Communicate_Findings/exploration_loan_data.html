Loan Data from Prosper¶

by Ailing Liu¶

Preliminary Wrangling¶

This data set is downloaded from Udacity. Originally it was from Prosper, Americas first marketplace lending platform, with over $9 billion in funded loans. This project is reasearching on the possible features that affect the borrowers APR or interest rate.

# import all packages and set plots to be embedded inline
import numpy as np
import pandas as pd
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sb
import urllib
%matplotlib inline
import os

# create sub folders for images, data
IMAGE_DIR = os.path.join('.', 'IMAGE')
DATA_DIR = os.path.join('.', 'DATA')
if not os.path.isdir(IMAGE_DIR):
    os.mkdir(IMAGE_DIR)
if not os.path.isdir(DATA_DIR):
    os.mkdir(DATA_DIR)

# save figure
def save_fig(name, path=IMAGE_DIR, dpi=300, format='png'):
    """Save figure into designatred foler, given figure name and path."""
    
    file_path = os.path.join(IMAGE_DIR, name+'.'+format)
    plt.savefig(file_path, dpi=dpi)

# fetch data from udacity amazon s3
def fetch_data(dataurl, filename, path=DATA_DIR, format='csv'):
    """Fetch data from the provided link. 
    Save the data with customized name and destination.
    """
    
    file_path = os.path.join(DATA_DIR, filename+'.'+format)
    urllib.request.urlretrieve(dataurl, file_path)
    print('Data downloaded in: ', file_path)

This dataset has 113937 entries, 81 columns. It consists of multiple data types, including numerical data, categorical data.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I am interested in the features that affect the borrowers APR or interest rate.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

The Prosper Score is expected to have negative relation with ARP, because higher rating reflects borrower's good credit history or lower risk. When borrower has strong credibility, the interest rate is low. Additionally, other factors such as borrower's occupation, origional load amount, contract term, monthly income may also affect their APR.

Discuss the distribution(s) of your variable(s) of interest. Were there any unusual points? Did you need to perform any transformations?¶

The distribution of APR looks normal except for a starggering peak round 0.35 to 0.37. A large peak centered at 0.2. Most of the values are at the range of 0.05 and 0.4. There are no unusual points and no need to perform any transformations.

Suprisingly, the richest person with 1.75 million income borrowed only 4000 from the loan institute. There are in total 9 persons have 100k income, but borrow for less than 5k. These data seemed to be mistake. In addition, the very rich people over 30k income are outliers in our dataset judged from the boxplot. To mitigate bias, we discard these outliers, overall 0.3% from our dataset.

Of the features you investigated, were there any unusual distributions? Did you perform any operations on the data to tidy, adjust, or change the form of the data? If so, why did you do this?¶

The distributions of stated monthly income is highly right screwed. Most stated monthly incomes are less than 30k. Surprisingly, there are 9 people with 100k+ monthly income only loan less than 5k dollars. This is more likely to be mistake in the income. We can either transform the income to square root, hoping it will become normal distribution for next step analysis. But it will also keep those mistakes in our data. So to mitigate bias, I discard those data whose income is large than 30k. Overall, only 0.3% of borrowers have started monthly income greater than 30k.

non_performing_mask = loan['LoanStatus'].isin(['Defaulted', 'Chargedoff'])
non_perf = loan[non_performing_mask].groupby('Occupation')['LoanStatus'].size().to_frame('NPF_Counts')

.join(income, how='outer').fillna(0)

occupation_loan_status['NPF%'] = occupation_loan_status['NPF_Counts'] / occupation_loan_status['Total']
